# Coastal Birds

## Core idea

Los Cóbanos is not only interesting below the waterline. The protected buffer zone also has bird records strong enough to support real natural-history copy.

## What the sources support

- A SciELO paper documented the first national record of `Psarocolius montezuma` in El Salvador from the buffer zone of Complejo Los Cóbanos.
- The Los Cóbanos management plan describes esteros and nearby habitats as holding threatened waterbirds and other coastal birdlife.
- A UES thesis on `Cacicus melanicterus` recorded reproductive biology in the terrestrial and buffer zones of the protected area.

## Why it matters for site copy

- The site can talk more confidently about birds, especially in mangrove and village-edge contexts.
- This supports the idea that Los Cóbanos is worth the stop even above water and even when offshore plans change.
- The national-first record is one of the clearest examples that the protected buffer zone matters biologically, not just visually.

## Good public phrasing

- Birdlife in Los Cóbanos is not filler around the reef. The protected buffer zone has produced nationally notable records, which tells you the habitat is doing real ecological work.
- If the sea is rough, the slower bird-and-shoreline version of Los Cóbanos is still a real nature day.
