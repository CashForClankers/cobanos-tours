# Intertidal Life

## Core idea

The shallow intertidal and reef-flat zone is one of the best-supported parts of the Los Cóbanos research record. It gives the site a stronger story than generic "tropical fish" language.

## What the sources support

- A 2011 UES thesis on the rocky intertidal fish community argues that this zone matters for the balance of fish populations in adjacent areas and was understudied in El Salvador.
- A 2017 UES thesis recorded 11 intertidal echinoderm species in the protected area, with `Ophiocoma aetiops` the most abundant in that study.
- A 2015 UES thesis recorded 9 opisthobranch species and 2,728 individuals across four monitoring beaches in the protected area.
- A 2017 UES fisheries thesis on `Lutjanus argentiventris` adds species-level detail on local fish biology, diet, and fishing pressure.

## Why it matters for site copy

- The strongest visitor-facing angle is abundance of small life in rocky intertidal habitat, not just headline megafauna.
- This supports tide-pool, shoreline, and reef-edge wording even on days when offshore plans change.

## Good public phrasing

- Even above the boat-and-snorkel layer, Los Cóbanos is full of intertidal life researchers keep measuring: fish, echinoderms, sea slugs, and reef-edge species adapted to rock, surge, and shallow water.
