# Protected Status

## Core idea

Los Cóbanos is not just pretty coastline. It is managed protected habitat with Ramsar significance, a large mostly marine footprint, and buffer-zone ecology that keeps showing up in research.

## What the sources support

- Ramsar Site `2419` confirms the `Complejo Los Cóbanos` and the `21,312 ha` area figure.
- The bird paper documenting the first national record of `Psarocolius montezuma` places the protected complex at `21,312 ha` with a strongly marine footprint and describes buffer-zone gallery forest and mangrove vegetation at Los Tubos.
- A 2025 UES internship report on mangrove restoration in the Los Cóbanos conservation area shows that restoration and salt-forest work are ongoing, not historical.

## Why it matters for site copy

- Protected status justifies the stronger language around wildlife, habitat respect, and slower observation.
- The buffer-zone story is useful because visitors often experience Los Cóbanos through trees, birds, iguanas, mangroves, and village edge before they ever get in the water.

## Good public phrasing

- Los Cóbanos works as a protected coastal system, not just a snorkeling pin on a map.
- The reef, mangroves, rivers, and village edge all belong to the same story.
