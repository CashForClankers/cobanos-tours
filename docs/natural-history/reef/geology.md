# Reef Geology

## Core idea

Los Cóbanos feels different because the coast is built on rocky volcanic substrate rather than a simple sand beach. That changes wave energy, tide-pool structure, shoreline texture, and where reef life can establish itself.

## What the sources support

- The MARN CBD report describes Los Cóbanos as volcanic rocky reef habitat.
- A 2016 hydrogeology paper for the Las Marías basin in Los Cóbanos used lithological, hydrochemical, and geophysical information to propose a conceptual aquifer model and rule out seawater intrusion in the study area.
- A 2004 UES macroalgae thesis describes Los Cóbanos as a rocky beach of volcanic origin with reef formation from 0 to 30 m depth.

## Why it matters for site copy

- "Rocky Pacific reef" is better than generic "beautiful beach."
- The geology explains why tide pools, volcanic rock, water shoes, and visibility caution all belong in the same story.
- The coast should be framed as textured and irregular, not flat and resort-like.

## Good public phrasing

- Los Cóbanos sits on rocky volcanic coast, which is why the reef, tide pools, and shoreline all feel more textured than a standard sand stop.
- The same geology that makes entry trickier is part of what makes the place worth seeing.
