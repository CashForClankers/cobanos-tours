# Iguanas

## Core idea

The large green iguanas around the meeting point are one of the easiest ways visitors feel the protected-area effect before the boat even leaves.

## What the sources support

- A 2018 UES herpetofauna thesis studied reptile and amphibian diversity plus threat factors in two coastal terrestrial ecosystems of the Los Cóbanos complex.
- Local reporting confirms iguanas and garrobos are still taken for sale and consumption in El Salvador despite wildlife-protection law.
- That makes it reasonable to explain the visible size and confidence of iguanas in Los Cóbanos as a protected-area and lower-hunting-pressure effect, but not as a measured population study.

## Why it matters for site copy

- This is a distinctive, concrete arrival detail.
- The strongest wording is about protection and lower hunting pressure, not sentimentality.
- Visitors do not need a long reptile lecture; they need one strong line that explains why the iguanas feel memorable here.

## Good public phrasing

- The big green iguanas around the trees and parking lot are one of the first signs that Los Cóbanos is protected habitat, not just another beach stop.
