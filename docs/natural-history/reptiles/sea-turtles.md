# Sea Turtles

## Core idea

Sea turtles belong in the Los Cóbanos natural-history story, but the public copy should stay cautious. The protected complex is turtle habitat; that does not mean visitors should expect a sighting.

## What the sources support

- The Los Cóbanos management plan lists four marine turtle species in the area: `Chelonia mydas`, `Eretmochelys imbricata`, `Lepidochelys olivacea`, and `Dermochelys coriacea`.
- The MARN CBD report supports using hawksbill turtle context in Los Cóbanos.
- The management plan also shows that turtle eggs and turtle-related use were relevant enough to appear in the local socioeconomic and management context.

## Why it matters for site copy

- Turtles make sense as protected-area context, not as a booking promise.
- Hawksbill is the strongest visitor-facing species to mention because it is well known and conservation-significant.
- This page should stay grounded in habitat and protection, not in encounter marketing.

## Good public phrasing

- Los Cóbanos is marine turtle habitat inside a protected coastal system, including hawksbill turtle context in the official environmental record.
- Treat turtles here as a conservation fact, not a guaranteed sighting.
