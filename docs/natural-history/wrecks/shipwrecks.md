# Shipwrecks

## Core idea

The wreck story at Los Cóbanos is stronger when treated as underwater history inside a protected marine area, not as generic adventure copy.

## What the sources support

- El Salvador Divers publicly lists the `SS Douglas` and `SS Cheribon` as Los Cóbanos dive sites.
- Visit El Salvador also places wreck exploration in the Los Cóbanos dive context and names `SS Douglas`, `SS Cheribon`, and `SS Arce`.
- La Prensa Gráfica reported three documented 19th-century wrecks within the protected Los Cóbanos area: `SS Columbus`, `Cheribon`, and `Douglas`.

## Why it matters for site copy

- The wrecks make Los Cóbanos more historically layered than a pure reef stop.
- They support diver and scuba-curious search intent without promising that every casual visitor will reach them.
- They also help connect reef tourism to underwater archaeology and local heritage.

## Good public phrasing

- Los Cóbanos is not only reef. It also holds documented 19th-century wrecks inside the protected area.
- Treat the wrecks as heritage first and dive context second.
