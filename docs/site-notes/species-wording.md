# Safe Species Wording

## Safe now

- `hawksbill turtle` in protected-area or habitat wording
- `olive ridley nesting` in internal or research-facing Los Cóbanos notes
- `leatherback habitat` inside the broader protected-complex context
- `giant seahorse reported in the Ramsar site overview` if the site ever needs that level of detail
- `dolphins` without species names
- `Stenella attenuata`, `Tursiops truncatus`, and `Stenella coeruleoalba` when the copy is explicitly tied to Los Cóbanos cetacean observations
- `marine turtle habitat` without promising sightings

## Keep conservative in public copy

- Prefer turtle habitat, protection, and lighting guidance over nesting-tour language.
- Keep whale and dolphin language seasonal and condition-dependent.
- Keep deep-habitat species off beginner-snorkel pages.
- Hawksbill is still the cleanest public-facing turtle species to foreground, even though olive-ridley nesting now has local support.

## Do not publish yet

- `hammerhead nursery` claims without a primary Los Cóbanos citation
