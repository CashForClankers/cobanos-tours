## Why this matters

This article is useful because it compresses the current bullish case into a simple investor narrative:
- security turnaround
- tourism growth
- Bitcoin branding and capital attention
- diaspora and return migration potential
- early-stage market upside

## What to keep

- The article correctly frames El Salvador as a high-conviction story rather than a mature, low-risk market.
- It is directionally useful on remittances, tourism, and the importance of political narrative.
- It is useful as a checklist of claims to test.

## What not to inherit blindly

- sweeping bullish framing
- hotel or real-estate upside assumptions without supply data
- soft claims about migration or demand without current statistics
- policy optimism without IMF / fiscal context

## Claims to verify

- tourism growth and whether it is broad or concentrated
- remittances as share of GDP
- fertility and population-growth constraints
- FDI strength versus narrative
- debt and financing constraints
- whether Bitcoin remains material for actual transaction flow or mostly branding
