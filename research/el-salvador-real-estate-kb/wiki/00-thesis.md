# Thesis Map

## Core Question

Is El Salvador real estate investable because fundamentals are already strong, or because narrative and selective demand are outrunning weak legacy constraints?

## Current answer

`Sourced`
- Security improvements changed the country's investability story.
- Tourism and remittances are both materially important.
- The IMF program shows that macro stabilization and governance reform are still active work, not solved problems.
- Population growth is modest and fertility is low, which weakens simplistic long-run housing-demand assumptions.

`Inference`
- The most investable segments are probably not "El Salvador real estate" in the abstract, but specific corridors where tourism, diaspora capital, and safety gains overlap.

`Needs verification`
- Which submarkets have genuine transaction depth rather than just attention.
- Whether hotel, branded stay, and short-term rental growth is translating into durable residential pricing power.

## Candidate submarkets to map next

- San Salvador urban core and upper-income neighborhoods
- La Libertad / Surf City corridor
- beach and tourism zones with foreign demand
- secondary cities with diaspora build activity
