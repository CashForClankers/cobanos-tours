# Macro

## Snapshot

`Sourced`
- World Bank data currently shows:
  - GDP growth `2.6%` in `2024`
  - population `6,338,193` in `2024`
  - population growth `0.5%` in `2024`
  - personal remittances received `24.0% of GDP` in `2024`
- World Bank fertility data shows total fertility rate at `1.8` in `2023`.
- IMF reports in `2025` describe the economy as expanding, but still under an active stabilization and reform program.
- The IMF's `40-month` EFF was approved on `February 26, 2025`.

`Inference`
- These are not crisis-collapse numbers, but they also do not justify a broad national boom thesis by themselves.
- Real-estate upside depends on segment-level demand more than headline growth.

## Implications for property

- Remittances can support housing demand even when local wage growth is weak.
- Low fertility and slow population growth argue against lazy "just buy because demographics" reasoning.
- Fiscal adjustment and external-risk sensitivity still matter for rates, liquidity, and investor sentiment.
