# Demand Drivers

## Tourism

`Sourced`
- Tourism has been one of the clearest growth stories in the country narrative.
- Local reporting citing official data says El Salvador reached roughly `4.0 million` international visitors in `2024`, up about `17.3%` versus `2023`.
- Local reporting also says January `2026` reached `413,000` international visitors, up `17%` year over year.

`Inference`
- Tourism likely matters most for:
  - hospitality assets
  - mixed-use or serviced-apartment plays
  - beach and surf corridors
  - land banking near infrastructure-backed leisure growth

## Remittances and diaspora

`Sourced`
- Remittances remain structurally huge.
- BCR publishes monthly remittance inflow and regional destination series, which means this can be localized better than most market commentary admits.

`Inference`
- Some residential demand is likely less about speculative investment and more about:
  - family-supported self-build
  - upgrade cycles
  - return migration
  - second-home or part-time occupancy patterns

## Security

`Sourced`
- World Bank overview language explicitly links the crime drop to stronger investor confidence.

`Inference`
- Security is not just a macro story. It changes:
  - willingness to travel
  - willingness to hold property
  - operating assumptions for rentals, retail, and hospitality
